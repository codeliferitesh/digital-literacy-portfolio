Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. 

In a typical phishing attack, a user receives an email or message that appears to be from a trusted source like a bank or a well-known company. The message often contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks real. When the user enters their details, the attacker gains access to their information.

Such attacks can lead to financial loss, identity theft, and data breaches. Many users fall victim because the messages look genuine and create a sense of urgency.
